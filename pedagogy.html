BCOBM214 · Pedagogy

How This Course Is Taught

An entrepreneurship course should feel like a venture — small bets, fast cycles, real audiences, honest feedback. The pedagogy below is the operating system that makes that possible across 10 units and 12 sessions.

The Pedagogical Spine

BCOBM214 is built on three interlocking layers:

LayerSourceWhat it does
Experiential cycleKolb (1984)Each session moves from concrete experience → reflection → conceptualisation → experimentation. No unit is purely lecture.
Effectual logicSarasvathy (2001)The course mirrors how expert entrepreneurs actually decide: bird-in-hand, affordable loss, lemonade, crazy quilt, pilot-in-the-plane.
Bloom's taxonomy ladderBloom et al. (1956); Anderson & Krathwohl (2001)Activities and assessment items are designed to climb from Remember/Understand toward Analyse/Evaluate/Create.

The 7-Phase Session Structure

Each 2.5-hour session is built from the same seven phases. The phase set is shared across all units; what changes is the signature pedagogy in the middle.

  1. OpenHook the room. A startling number, a quick poll, or a one-minute anecdote that makes today's question feel personal.
  2. FramePre-reading recap and the unit's framing question. Students restate it in their own words.
  3. ExploreFramework gallery walk — students browse the unit's 8 frameworks and pick which they expect to use.
  4. ApplyThe signature pedagogy of the unit (Presentation, Case Study, Role Play, Hackathon or Business Challenge).
  5. SynthesiseGroups translate insight into one slide / one sketch / one decision recommendation.
  6. ReflectTwo-minute paired share or one-word exit ticket. The Kolb "Reflective Observation" stage.
  7. ClosePreview next session, give the pre-reading hook, and assign the reflection prompt for independent learning.

The Five Signature Pedagogies

Each unit's "Apply" phase uses a different EU Business School signature pedagogy. The five formats are taken directly from the EUBS approach to Bachelor- and MBA-level teaching, and are scaffolded across the course so every student practises each format at least twice (and the capstone integrates all of them).

PedagogyWhat students doUsed inSkills it builds
PresentationPresent a strategic proposal — vision, plan or pitch — under time pressure.Units 1, 9Communication · Persuasion · Synthesis
Case StudyConnect data and frameworks to a managerial decision in a specific case.Units 2, 6Critical analysis · Decision-making
Role PlayTake on a role in a simulated business scenario or panel debate.Units 3, 7Empathy · Negotiation · Listening
HackathonDevelop an innovative proposal under constraint and defend it in a pitch.Units 4, 5, 8Speed · Constraint design · Risk-taking

Avoiding Duplication with Sister Courses

Boundaries

Business Management: strategic planning and organisational behaviour are touched only insofar as they shape entrepreneurial decisions. We don't repeat OB theory — we apply it to founder-led teams.

Marketing & UX: marketing strategies and segmentation are explicitly framed through a startup / new-venture lens (e.g. Lean Canvas instead of full marketing plan).

How Independent Learning is Structured

Of the 130 ECTS hours, 30 are in class. The remaining 100 are scaffolded as follows:

ActivityHoursForm
Reading25Pre-readings + textbook chapters (~5 pages/hour)
Assessment 1 prep (Exam)20Reflection prompts, framework recall practice, mock items
Assessment 2 prep (Business Plan)25Lean Canvas → Plan draft → Peer review → Final pitch
Individual / group study30Discussion forums, customer interviews, group meetings

Assessment Design

Both assessments are constructively aligned (Biggs, 1996) — every assessment item maps back to a stated Intended Learning Outcome.

AssessmentWeightILOsFormat
Exam40%2, 3, 4Mixed: MCQ · Short answer · Mini case
Business Plan + Pitch60%1, 2, 3, 5Written plan + 8-min pitch in Unit 10 capstone

Group work norms

Group work is optional. Where chosen, peer evaluation accompanies the submission so individual contribution is visible. Working solo is fully supported — Sarasvathy's Effectuation works at any team size.
