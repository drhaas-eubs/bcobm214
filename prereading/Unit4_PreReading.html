BCOBM214 · Unit 4 · Pre-Reading

From Idea to Plan in 90 Minutes

Why the first version of any plan should be drafted under time pressure — and why what survives the constraint is the only part that matters. Read before Session 4. ~12 minutes.

Why this reading matters

Most business plans take weeks to write and never get read. The mistake is treating the plan as a deliverable rather than as a thinking artefact. The discipline this unit teaches is the opposite: a 90-minute hackathon to draft a complete one-page strategy, then iterate it under live constraint. What survives that compression is your real strategy. Everything else is decoration.

The 90-Minute Strategy Skeleton

Three frameworks together produce a complete first-draft strategy in 90 minutes:

FrameworkTimeWhat it answers
F25 Business Model Canvas20 minHow does the business actually create, deliver and capture value?
F28 Strategy Diamond20 minWhere do we play, with what vehicles, differentiated how, in what sequence, with what economics?
F32 Bowling Alley20 minWhich one segment do we dominate first, and what comes next?

The remaining 30 minutes go to pitch craft and review.

Why constraint is the friend of strategy

The trap of unlimited time

Given unlimited time, founders accumulate options. Plans grow until every conceivable customer, channel and product is mentioned. The result is unfundable and unexecutable — strategy is at heart a series of refusals, not additions.

Constraint forces refusal. A 20-minute timer on the Business Model Canvas means you can't draw it twice; you have to pick one customer segment, one value proposition, one revenue model. That commitment is the strategy.

Three frameworks to bring to class

  1. Business Model Canvas (F25) — be ready to fill all 9 blocks in 20 minutes
  2. Strategy Diamond (F28) — be ready to answer all 5 questions in plain language
  3. Bowling Alley (F32) — be ready to pick a beachhead segment and defend it

If you can't recall the components of any of these, re-visit their Slibrary sheets in the Unit 4 framework gallery.

Connection to ILOs

Today's pre-reading and tomorrow's hackathon support ILO 1 (Develop a comprehensive business plan) and ILO 4 (Distinguish business development from other functions). The hackathon's pace forces students to inhabit BD's role: integrating market insight, strategy and operations under live time pressure.

Reading prompt

If you had 90 minutes to convince an investor to fund your venture, what would you cut from your current pitch?
