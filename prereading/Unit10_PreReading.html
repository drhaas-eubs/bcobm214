BCOBM214 · Unit 10 · Capstone Pre-Reading

What Investors Read in 90 Seconds

Synthesis of the entire course from the receiving side — what the panel will actually look for in your capstone pitch. Read before the capstone session. ~12 minutes.

The single shift this reading asks of you

For nine units, you have been the founder. For Unit 10's preparation, become the panel. The shift in perspective is what separates capstone pitches that earn FUND verdicts from those that earn KILL.

Investors do not read pitches the way founders write them. They run a fast pattern-match against five lenses (F74), they form a 90-second hypothesis (Unit 9 pre-reading), and they spend the remaining 18 minutes either confirming or weakening it. The most common mistake in capstone is to write a pitch that is internally satisfying to the founder but does not address what the panel actually weighs.

The Five Lenses, in panel-order weight

Lens (F74)QuestionWhat weakens the lens fastest
TractionIs there evidence of pull?Time spent building without paying customers; metrics without behavioural meaning
TeamCan these people execute?Founders who haven't done the customer work themselves; missing critical roles
MarketIs it large and reachable?Inflated TAM; no bottom-up SOM; ambiguous customer segment
ProductDoes it work and is it differentiated?Vague defensibility; no answer to 'why doesn't an incumbent build this?'
DealDo the terms work?Asking for too much without milestone-tied tranches; valuation incoherent with stage

The integration test

The panel will not test your knowledge of any one framework. They will test whether your decisions cohere across the entire venture. Three integration questions matter most:

  1. Does your customer evidence match your strategy? If you've discovered through Unit 3 customer interviews that your customer is X, does your Unit 4 BMC's customer segment block actually say X?
  2. Does your funding ask match your venture's economics? If your unit economics from Unit 8 imply a bootstrapped path, are you asking for VC money? Why?
  3. Does your team match your plan? If your plan requires international expansion in Year 2, does your team include anyone with international scale-up experience?

Three things the panel notices in 90 seconds

1. Founder presence

The panel can tell within 30 seconds whether the founder believes their own pitch. Conviction without arrogance is the rarest combination — and the most valued.

2. Honest acknowledgement of the riskiest assumption

Pitches that pre-empt the panel's hardest question always score better than pitches that hide it. "The biggest risk in this plan is X. Here's how we plan to test it in the first 90 days" is one of the strongest possible openings.

3. Coherence between pitch and submitted plan

The panel will have read the plan. Pitches that contradict the plan, or cherry-pick favourable elements, are immediately downgraded.

The capstone day itself

You have 5 minutes to deliver the pitch + 3 minutes for Q&A. Three suggestions for the morning:

  • Sleep more than the night before. Cognitive load matters more than memorisation.
  • Power-pose for 2 minutes outside the room (F72). The behavioural effect is real.
  • Re-read your favourite framework Slibrary sheet right before walking in. It anchors you in the discipline you've built across the course.

Connection to ILOs

The capstone tests all five ILOs simultaneously. The pre-reading is the reminder of what integration looks like from the receiving side.

Reading prompt

If you were on the panel for your own pitch, scoring honestly, what verdict would you give yourself — and what would you change in the next 7 days to earn a different verdict?
