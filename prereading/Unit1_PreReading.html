The Effectuation Mindset

Why expert entrepreneurs don't predict the future — they co-create it. Read before Session 1. ~15 minutes.

Why this reading matters

Most introductory entrepreneurship courses begin with a story about a single visionary founder who saw the future before everyone else. That story sells books. It does not, however, describe how most successful entrepreneurs actually start.

In a now-famous study, Saras Sarasvathy (2001) interviewed 27 expert entrepreneurs — each of whom had built at least one company past $200M — and gave them an identical entrepreneurial problem. She watched what they did. The pattern was striking. They almost never began with prediction. They began with whatever was already in front of them, and they expanded outward through commitments from people who chose to come along.

She called this logic effectuation, and contrasted it with the causal logic that business plans are usually written in.

The Five Principles

PrincipleOne-line summaryThe "I" question
Bird-in-HandStart with who you are, what you know, and whom you know.What means do I already have?
Affordable LossDecide what you can afford to lose, not what you might gain.What's the worst case I can live with?
LemonadeTreat surprises as material — leverage them rather than avoiding them.How can this surprise become an asset?
Crazy QuiltBuild with anyone willing to commit, not with anyone you wish would commit.Who already wants to play with me?
Pilot-in-the-PlaneThe future is shaped, not forecast — keep the entrepreneur at the controls.What can I do, today, that nudges the future I want?

Key framework for this Unit

F4 · Effectuation Principles

You will use this framework in Session 1's vision pitch and revisit it in Unit 10's capstone. Most other course frameworks (Lean Startup, JTBD, Customer Development, the Mom Test) are downstream descendants of effectual logic.

How effectuation differs from causal logic

Causal logicEffectual logic
GoalGiven. Choose means to achieve it.Discovered. Means available shape the goal.
View of riskManage expected return.Manage downside (affordable loss).
View of othersCompetitors to beat.Stakeholders to enrol.
View of the futureTo be predicted.To be co-created.

Connection to ILOs

Effectuation supports ILO 3 (Critically review the key aspect of entrepreneurship). It contrasts with the standard MBA "opportunity recognition then resource acquisition" model — and the contrast itself is the learning.

Reflection prompt to come prepared with

If you had to start a business tonight, with only what you already have within your reach, what is the smallest possible business you could be running by Sunday?

Source: Sarasvathy, S. D. (2001) Causation and Effectuation: Toward a Theoretical Shift from Economic Inevitability to Entrepreneurial Contingency. Academy of Management Review, 26(2). Read also: Read, S., Sarasvathy, S., Dew, N. & Wiltbank, R. (2017) Effectual Entrepreneurship. Routledge.
