BCOBM214 · Unit 2 · Case Study

Beans & Bytes — Three Ideas, One Founder

A teaching case for Unit 2's Idea Triage. The point is not to find the right idea — it is to learn how to tell a real one from a fashionable one.

Background

Marius Albu is a 24-year-old computer science graduate from Bucharest now studying at EU Business School Geneva. He spent his summers running a small specialty coffee stand at music festivals across Romania, where he developed a reputation for being faster than every competitor at the same price point — because he had built his own queue-management software on a tablet during his second year of university.

By the start of his Year 2 BCOBM, Marius has narrowed his entrepreneurial ambitions to three candidate ideas. He has six weeks before he must commit to one for his Year 2 business plan.

Idea 1 — Speciality coffee subscription for Geneva students

A €19/month subscription delivering 250g of small-batch roasted beans, plus weekly brewing tips, to student dorms. He estimates 4,000 student coffee drinkers in Geneva willing to pay €19/month. He has a friend who roasts in Lausanne who could supply at €7.50/kg.

Idea 2 — Queue-management SaaS for festival food vendors

A licensed version of the software he built for himself, sold as €49/month per stand to small festival food traders across Europe. He estimates 18,000 small festival food vendors in Europe and three direct competitors at €120+/month. His software runs on cheap Android tablets, theirs requires dedicated POS hardware.

Idea 3 — A bean-to-cup coffee shop on the EU campus

A single-location coffee shop on the Geneva campus, in a corner that is currently a vending machine. He has had one informal conversation with the campus operations director, who said "if it's well-run, we'd consider it." He estimates 600 daily transactions at €4 average ticket.

The Trap

Marius's heart is on Idea 3. His head says Idea 2. His effectuation principles say Idea 1. He cannot tell which voice is right.

Your Task

You are Marius's triage panel.

  1. Apply F11 Lean Canvas in 5 minutes per idea. Note the box that is least filled in for each.
  2. Apply F12 JTBD per idea: who hires this product, and for what job?
  3. Apply F4 Affordable Loss from Unit 1: which idea has a survivable downside?
  4. Make a verdict for each idea: GO / KILL / PIVOT.
  5. Write Marius a 3-sentence WhatsApp message telling him your verdict and why.

Watch out for

The "obviously bigger market" idea (SaaS, 18,000 vendors in Europe) is rarely the obvious right answer for a 24-year-old with a Year 2 deadline. Bigger markets mean more competition and longer cycles. The right answer is the idea where the affordable loss is acceptable and the customer is reachable in 7 days.

Source: Teaching case developed for BCOBM214 by Dr. Hildegard Haas. Beans & Bytes is fictional; vendor count and pricing inspired by ESN Romania, EFFA festival research and Lausanne micro-roastery interviews.
