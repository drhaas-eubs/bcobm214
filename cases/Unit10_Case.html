Demo Day — Six Plans, Six Verdicts

A composite teaching case for Unit 10 — six anonymised pitches from previous capstones, with the panel's actual verdicts and the patterns that distinguished them.

The Setup

The following six pitch summaries are composites drawn from BCOBM214 capstone Demo Days across 2023, 2024 and 2025. Names and details have been anonymised. The verdicts are real — drawn from panel scoring sheets and the external investors' written debriefs.

Pitch A — "FleetFresh" (Cold-chain logistics SaaS)

ElementWhat was pitched
CustomerMid-sized European pharmaceutical distributors
ProductIoT-enabled temperature monitoring + compliance reporting
Traction3 letters of intent, no paying customers, 18 months building
DealSeeking €1.5M seed at €6M pre

Panel verdict: KILL. Scoring: Team 4 · Market 4 · Product 3 · Traction 1 · Deal 3. Written feedback: "Strong founders, real market, plausible product. But 18 months and zero paying customers means either the customer doesn't actually want this, or the team can't sell. Either way, not yet investable. Get one paying pilot before raising."

Pitch B — "Maris" (Sustainable swimwear)

ElementWhat was pitched
CustomerEco-conscious women, 25-45, EU markets
ProductRecycled-ocean-plastic swimwear, premium positioning
Traction€340k revenue Year 1, 8% repeat purchase rate
DealSeeking €600k angel for inventory + marketing

Panel verdict: WAIT. Scoring: Team 3 · Market 3 · Product 4 · Traction 3 · Deal 2. Written feedback: "Real product, real revenue, but the 8% repeat rate is the killer signal. €600k of marketing won't fix retention. Spend the next quarter understanding why customers don't come back, then come back with a stronger story."

Pitch C — "Vento" (B2B wind-turbine maintenance software)

ElementWhat was pitched
CustomerEuropean wind-turbine operators (15+ turbines)
ProductPredictive-maintenance ML + workflow management
Traction2 paying customers (€8k/month each), 4 in pipeline
DealSeeking €2M seed at €8M pre, building European salesforce

Panel verdict: FUND. Scoring: Team 5 · Market 4 · Product 4 · Traction 4 · Deal 3. Written feedback: "All 5 lenses pass. Founders have domain depth (one is ex-Vestas). Two paying customers at €8k/month is real signal. Pipeline is credible. The €8M pre-money is a stretch but workable. Lead this round."

Pitch D — "Habit" (Behaviour-change app for diabetes)

ElementWhat was pitched
CustomerType 2 diabetes patients
ProductApp + coaching for habit formation
Traction1,200 free users, 3% conversion to paid (€9.99/month)
DealSeeking €1.2M to scale paid acquisition

Panel verdict: WAIT. Scoring: Team 3 · Market 5 · Product 3 · Traction 2 · Deal 2. Written feedback: "Huge market. Real founder mission (founder's parent has Type 2). But 3% conversion at €9.99 means lifetime value is €60-€80 max. Customer acquisition cost will exceed that. Either fix the price (B2B insurance channel?) or the product (does it actually move A1c?) before scaling."

Pitch E — "Carta Blanca" (Curated wine subscription, similar to Aria from Unit 5)

ElementWhat was pitched
CustomerAffluent Swiss wine drinkers, premium positioning
ProductCurated boxes from small Italian producers
Traction180 subscribers in 4 months, 1.4% conversion from web traffic
DealSeeking €450k seed

Panel verdict: KILL. Scoring: Team 4 · Market 2 · Product 4 · Traction 2 · Deal 2. Written feedback: "Lovely product. But the unit economics of DTC wine subscription are well-understood and brutal — Aria Wines failed for the same reasons. The honest pitch would address how this differs from the dozens that failed before. The pitch did not."

Pitch F — "Triage" (Clinical decision support for ER doctors)

ElementWhat was pitched
CustomerHospital emergency departments
ProductAI-powered triage decision support, integrated with hospital EHRs
Traction1 hospital pilot signed, 24-month sales cycle ahead
DealSeeking €4M Series A to fund regulatory clearance + 3 hospital deployments

Panel verdict: FUND (with conditions). Scoring: Team 5 · Market 4 · Product 4 · Traction 3 · Deal 4. Written feedback: "Domain-deep founders, real hospital pilot, regulatory pathway clear. The 24-month sales cycle is the risk — funding needs to be tranched. Approve €1.5M now, additional €2.5M on regulatory milestone. Lead this with Series A specialist."

The Patterns

Across the six verdicts, three patterns dominated:

  1. Traction is the most-weighted lens. Pitches with weak traction (FleetFresh, Habit) were killed or paused regardless of other lens strength. Pitches with even modest traction (Vento, Triage) found support.
  2. Honesty about risk is a positive signal. Triage explicitly acknowledged the 24-month sales cycle and asked for tranched funding to address it. The honest framing produced the FUND verdict.
  3. Lack of differentiation from prior failures kills. Carta Blanca made no reference to the well-documented failure modes of DTC wine. The panel concluded that either the team didn't know or didn't want to acknowledge — both were disqualifying.

Your Task

You are preparing your own capstone pitch for the panel. Using the six pitches above:

  1. Score your own draft pitch on the 5-Lens Diligence (F74) — Team · Market · Product · Traction · Deal — as honestly as you would score someone else's
  2. Identify the lens where your pitch is currently weakest (this is almost certainly Traction or Deal)
  3. What can you do in the next 7 days to strengthen that lens before the capstone?
  4. Write yourself a one-sentence reminder that you will read 10 minutes before walking into your capstone pitch

The teaching point

Capstone is not a test of memory. It is a test of integration: can you stand in front of three people who evaluate ventures for a living, and own your decisions about what to build, why, with whom and how. Honesty is the most underrated pitch attribute. Bring it.
