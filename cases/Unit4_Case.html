Volta Mobility — Choosing a Go-to-Market

A teaching case for Unit 4 — used to test whether students can choose one go-to-market path under realistic constraint, and defend it.

The Concept

Volta Mobility is a Lisbon-based startup founded in late 2025 by two engineers and a former bicycle-shop owner. Their product is a battery-swap-on-demand service for delivery riders using e-bikes. Riders arrive at one of Volta's small (8m²) automated swap stations, scan their app, and exchange a depleted battery for a fully-charged one in under 90 seconds. Volta owns and maintains the batteries, charges riders €1.20 per swap, and earns secondary revenue from optimising charging during off-peak grid hours.

The Validated Insight

Six months of customer discovery (Unit 3 frameworks, applied) confirmed three things:

  • Lisbon's last-mile delivery riders waste 35-50 minutes per shift charging or finding a charged battery
  • The two largest delivery platforms (Glovo and Bolt Food) have ~3,400 active e-bike riders in Lisbon
  • Riders earn ~€11/hour; saving 30 minutes per shift is worth ~€5.50 to them — far more than €1.20 per swap

Volta has built and tested a single prototype station. It works. They have €120,000 of seed capital and a 12-month runway.

The Decision

Volta now faces a strategic-direction choice. Three plausible go-to-market paths sit on the table:

The Internal Debate

"Path A gets us to revenue fastest but burns most of our seed money on station infrastructure. Path B is the most strategic — exclusive contracts with platforms would lock out competitors — but five months without revenue might break us. Path C is the biggest prize but we'd have to fundraise again before we ever shipped."

Your Task

You are Volta's strategic advisor. Using three frameworks from Unit 4, prepare:

  1. A Business Model Canvas (F25) for the path you recommend
  2. An answer to all five Strategy Diamond (F28) questions for that path
  3. A Bowling Alley sequence (F32) showing which segment Volta dominates first and what comes next
  4. A 60-second pitch defending the choice — including what Volta will not do

Reading Hints

The trap

The "obviously biggest market" answer (Path C) is rarely the right answer for a 12-month-runway startup. The discipline is in matching capital, time and defensibility to your actual constraint — and being honest about the constraint.
