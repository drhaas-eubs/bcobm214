Casa de Mar — Why Entrepreneurs Quit Day Jobs

A short teaching case for Unit 1 — used to anchor the question: what is the trigger that turns an idea into a business?

The Situation

Lucía Fernández, 31, has spent eight years as a senior strategy consultant at a Madrid firm. Her parents — first-generation immigrants from Galicia — ran a small fishmonger in the same neighbourhood for twenty-two years before retiring last summer. Lucía grew up cleaning ice from the display every Saturday morning.

In November 2025, after a routine project review with a regional grocery chain, Lucía realised something her client did not: Spanish consumers under 35 buy almost no fresh fish. The category data showed a 41% decline among 18–35 year-olds in five years, while sales of fresh fish in branded restaurants and high-end takeaway concepts were up 28% in the same window. The problem was not demand for fish. It was the fishmonger format.

By December, Lucía had sketched a concept she called Casa de Mar: a chain of small, design-led "modern fishmongers" that would sell fresh fish portion-cut, vacuum-sealed, with one-card recipes, in walkable urban locations. The concept fused her parents' product knowledge with her own consultant's eye for category whitespace.

By January 2026, she had:

  • €38,000 in personal savings;
  • An informal commitment of €40,000 from her uncle Tomás (also a former fishmonger);
  • A friend in retail design who would help with branding "for cost";
  • One verbal expression of interest from a small commercial landlord she met at a wedding.

She also had a permanent contract earning €82,000 a year, an apartment with a three-year mortgage, and a partner who is supportive but anxious about losing the second income.

Lucía's Internal Debate

"Every consultant I know has a 'one day' business. Mine isn't 'one day' — it's literally my parents' product, repackaged for my generation. The market data is unambiguous. But every honest founder I've spoken to has said the same thing: it doesn't matter how good the idea is; what matters is whether I am the right person to start it."

Your Task

You are Lucía's friend from MBA days, calling her on a Sunday evening. She wants to talk through whether she should resign in February. Using two frameworks from Unit 1, prepare:

  1. A diagnosis of where Lucía is, in entrepreneurial terms (Schumpeter, Kirzner, Drucker or Sarasvathy lens — pick one).
  2. A vision recommendation using Sinek's Golden Circle, BHAG, or Strategic Intent — pick one.
  3. A short, honest assessment: should she resign in February, March, or not at all? Justify with reference to F4 Effectuation (specifically affordable loss).

Reading Hints

Watch for

This case is designed to test whether students mistake opportunity for readiness. Lucía has a real opportunity. The interesting question is whether she has the means (bird-in-hand) and an acceptable downside (affordable loss).

Source: Teaching case developed for BCOBM214 by Dr. Hildegard Haas. Casa de Mar is a fictional venture; market data inspired by Spanish Ministry of Agriculture and Eurostat 2024–25 fish consumption studies.
