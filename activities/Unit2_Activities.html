BCOBM214 · Unit 2 · Session Activity

Idea Triage — Case Conference

Signature pedagogy: Case Study. Today, students sit not as observers of an idea but as a triage panel deciding which ideas survive contact with reality.

Phase-by-Phase Facilitation

  1. Open · 10 minShow the class three real product launches that failed (e.g. Quibi, Juicero, Google Glass consumer edition). One sentence each. Ask: "What do these have in common?" Land the answer: they were not lacking effort or money — they were lacking a customer with a real job-to-be-done.
  2. Frame · 15 minWalk students through F12 JTBD using a familiar example (e.g. milkshakes for the morning commute). Then introduce F11 Lean Canvas with one minute on each box. Make explicit: today we test ideas by interrogating problem · customer · job, not by polishing solutions.
  3. Explore · 15 minEach student opens their own Reflection 1 from last week and pulls forward the three candidate business ideas they brought. Solo: rank them gut-feel from 1 (best) to 3 (worst). Note the ranking — we'll revisit it at the end.
  4. Apply · 70 min — The Triage PanelStudents form triads. In each triad:

    · Round 1 (20 min): Student A's three ideas. Students B and C act as "investor panel" using a triage protocol: 4 minutes per idea — A pitches in 60 seconds, B and C drill in for 3 minutes using Lean Canvas + JTBD. Verdict: GO / KILL / PIVOT.
    · Round 2 (20 min): Student B's three ideas. Same protocol.
    · Round 3 (20 min): Student C's three ideas. Same protocol.
    · Wrap-up (10 min): Each student picks one idea — not necessarily their original favourite — to take into Unit 3 customer discovery.
  5. Synthesise · 20 minPlenary. Each student names: (a) the idea they came in with as #1, (b) the idea they're leaving with, (c) which framework triggered the change. Faculty surfaces patterns. Common pattern: ideas survive because the customer is more clearly defined, not because the idea itself was better.
  6. Reflect · 10 minTwo prompts in writing:
    · What was the hardest "kill" verdict to give a peer? Why?
    · What would have changed your mind on your own #1 idea earlier?
  7. Close · 10 minPreview Unit 3 (Customer Discovery role play). Students assign themselves as customer hunters: by next session, they must have spoken — in person, by phone or by video — to two potential customers about the surviving idea. Pre-reading: Why Founders Talk to Strangers.

The Triage Protocol

Use this card during Round 1

60-second pitch: What's the idea? Who is it for? What job are they hiring it to do?
3-minute drill (panel):
1. Who is the customer (specific, not segment)?
2. What were they doing instead? Why was that not enough?
3. What's the simplest test you could run in 7 days?
4. What's the affordable loss to find out?
Verdict: GO (worth 7 days) · KILL (the customer isn't there) · PIVOT (the job is real but the solution is wrong).
