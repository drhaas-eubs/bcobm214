Plan Build Lab — Hackathon Session Guide

Signature pedagogy: Hackathon. Today, every pod converts last week's Business Model Canvas into a structured business plan — and stress-tests it against six recurring patterns of failure.

Phase-by-Phase Facilitation

  1. Open · 10 minHook: Show three real failed startups (e.g. Quibi, Beepi, Theranos) and their seed-stage pitch decks (one slide each). Ask: "Was the original plan wrong, or did execution fail it?" Land: most plans are not killed by competitors — they are killed by patterns the founder could have foreseen.
  2. Frame · 15 minFaculty draws Sahlman's 4-element frame (F33) on the board: People · Opportunity · Context · Risk & Reward. Then introduces Eisenmann's six failure patterns (F37) as the audit lens. Today's hackathon will produce a plan and stress-test it against the patterns.
  3. Explore · 10 minStudents individually browse the Unit 5 framework gallery. Pick the three frameworks they expect to lean on most. Everyone reads F37 (Eisenmann's six patterns) carefully — it will return in Round 3.
  4. Apply · 70 min — The Plan Build LabSame pods as Unit 4. Each pod brings their BMC from Unit 4 plus the case material.

    Round 1 (25 min): Draft the four Sahlman sections. People (real CVs, skin in the game), Opportunity (bottom-up sized), Context (timing, regulation, capital climate), Risk & Reward (honest distribution of outcomes).
    Round 2 (20 min): Add the operational layer — 3-5 SMART goals (F8/F34), 1 quarterly OKR set (F35), choose your North Star Metric (F40).
    Round 3 (25 min): Run the Eisenmann Audit. Score the plan 1-5 on vulnerability to each of the six failure patterns. Identify the worst pattern. Rewrite one section to address it.
  5. Synthesise · 20 minPod-to-pod peer review. Each pod hands its plan to the next pod, who runs an independent Eisenmann Audit. Findings reported in 3 minutes per pod (10 min total). Plenary debrief: where did peer audits surface what self-audits missed?
  6. Reflect · 10 minSolo writing:
    · Which of Eisenmann's six patterns is your venture most vulnerable to?
    · What in the plan is currently masking that vulnerability?
    · What would you change before submitting this plan to a real investor?
  7. Close · 15 minFaculty preview Unit 6 (Intrapreneurship case conference). Pre-reading: Why big firms hire entrepreneurs. Bring your final plan draft for use in Unit 10 Capstone.

Differentiation Notes

For pods that produced thin BMCs in Unit 4

Spend the first 25 minutes deepening the BMC before starting Sahlman. A weak canvas produces a weak Sahlman; better to fix it than to paper over it.

For confident pods

Layer in F38 (Bhide's Hustle as Strategy) and F39 (Innovator's Dilemma) — does your plan rely on hustle or structure for advantage? Is your firm a candidate for being disrupted by what you're building?
