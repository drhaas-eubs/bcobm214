BCOBM214 · Unit 10 · Capstone Session

Capstone — Business Challenge Session Guide

Signature pedagogy: Business Challenge. Today, every student integrates the entire course — vision, evidence, plan, financials, pitch — into a single 8-minute presentation in front of a guest investor panel. This is Assessment 2 (60% of the grade) made live.

The Format

DeckUp to 10 slides (Kawasaki F65); 30-pt minimum font; one core message per slide

Panel3 people: course facilitator + 1 EUBS faculty + 1 external guest (founder, angel, or VC)

Phase-by-Phase Facilitation

  1. Open · 10 minFaculty welcomes the guest panellist. Frames the session: "Today is the integration of everything we have done across 28 hours. Each of you will deliver an 8-minute window in front of three people whose job is to evaluate ventures professionally. There are no hidden tests. The frameworks you have been taught are the same frameworks the panel will use to listen." Briefly walk through the 5-Lens scorecard (F74) so students know how they will be assessed.
  2. Frame · 10 minShow the running order. Pitch order is randomised; a 4-minute changeover between pitches gives next presenter time to set up. Remind students of the Sahlman frame (F33) as the architectural backbone they have been building toward.
  3. Explore · 5 minQuiet preparation time. Students settle. Power-posing space available outside the room (F72). Faculty distributes peer-feedback scorecards to all observing students.
  4. Apply · 96 min — The Capstone Pitches12 students × 8 minutes each = 96 minutes. Each pitch:
    · 5 minutes uninterrupted presentation (timer projected on screen)
    · 3 minutes Q&A from the panel
    · No time extensions — discipline is part of the grade

    Panel scoring is private (collected at end). Peer scoring is anonymised.
  5. Synthesise · 15 minPanel reflects publicly: which patterns did they notice across 12 pitches? What separated the strongest from the weakest? Faculty surfaces themes — typically: clarity of customer, honesty of risk, coherence between plan and pitch, presence of the founder.
  6. Reflect · 10 minSolo writing using Kolb's cycle (F79):
    · Concrete experience: What just happened in your pitch?
    · Reflective observation: What are you noticing about yourself, your venture, your work across 28 hours?
    · Abstract conceptualisation: What general lesson are you taking forward?
    · Active experimentation: What will you do in the next 30 days that this course has changed?
  7. Close · 4 minFaculty closes with Sarasvathy's Pilot-in-the-Plane principle (F80): "Whatever happens next, you stay at the controls. You don't predict the future of your venture; you co-create it with the stakeholders willing to come along. Today was a 5-minute window. Your real venture life is unbounded. Go shape it."

Grading Notes

How the panel scores

The 5-Lens scorecard (F74) provides 25 points: Team (1-5) · Market (1-5) · Product (1-5) · Traction (1-5) · Deal (1-5). The submitted business plan provides up to 35 points (Sahlman's four sections × ILO alignment). Total Assessment 2 = 60% of course grade.

What makes the difference

Across years of capstone observation, the difference between average and excellent pitches is rarely the data. It is the integration: does the founder show that they understand why their plan works, and what they would do if any one element failed?
