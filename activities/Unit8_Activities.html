Funding Constraint Cards — Hackathon Session Guide

Signature pedagogy: Hackathon. Today, every pod designs a 24-month funding strategy for their venture under a randomly-drawn financial constraint — and learns that the right capital partner is rarely the obvious one.

Phase-by-Phase Facilitation

  1. Open · 10 minHook: Show two real funding stories side by side: Mailchimp (bootstrapped to $12B exit, no outside capital) and WeWork (raised $22B+, lost most of it). Ask: "What's the actual goal of fundraising?" Land: capital is a tool, not a trophy. Wrong capital can kill ventures faster than no capital.
  2. Frame · 15 minFaculty draws the Angel-VC-PE Spectrum (F60) and the Funding Curve (F61) on the board. Reviews the Cap Table mechanics (F62) and Pre-/Post-Money math (F63) briefly. Today: each pod designs a 24-month funding strategy under a random constraint.
  3. Explore · 10 minStudents individually browse the Unit 8 framework gallery for 8 minutes. Pick the three frameworks they expect to lean on most.
  4. Apply · 70 min — The Funding SprintForm 4 pods of 3 (use the same pods from Units 4-5 so students fund their own venture).

    Step 1 (5 min): Each pod draws a constraint card at random:
    · "Your founder team has just gone through a relationship breakdown — co-founder is leaving"
    · "Your sector just lost its primary VC fund — no European VCs are investing in it for 18 months"
    · "Regulatory uncertainty has killed your B2B sales channel for 12 months"
    · "A celebrity has tweeted about you positively — you have 30 days of viral interest with no infrastructure to capture it"
    · "Your largest customer is delaying payment by 90 days, draining your runway"
    · "An angel investor wants to invest €600k but only at a €15M cap that you think is too high"

    Step 2 (35 min): Design a 24-month funding strategy responsive to the constraint. Specify: which funding sources, which sequence, how much, with what dilution, what milestones to unlock the next round.

    Step 3 (20 min): Build the cap table forward through the 24 months. Show founder ownership at month 0, 12, 24.

    Step 4 (10 min): Draft a 90-second pitch that addresses the constraint without mentioning it explicitly.
  5. Synthesise · 20 minPlenary. Each pod delivers a 4-minute slot: 90-second pitch (without revealing constraint), then 2 minutes for the class to guess what the constraint card said. Reveal at the end. Faculty captures patterns: which constraints produced creative solutions vs. forced compromises?
  6. Reflect · 10 minSolo writing:
    · What did your constraint force you to consider that you wouldn't have considered without it?
    · If your constraint had been less severe, would your funding plan have been better or worse?
    · What constraint, if you faced it in real life, would most change your willingness to take VC?
  7. Close · 15 minFaculty preview Unit 9 (Pitch Craft Studio). Pre-reading: Why investors stop listening. Bring the constraint-aware pitch from today for refinement.
