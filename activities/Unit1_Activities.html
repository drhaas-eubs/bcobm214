Vision Pitch — 7-Phase Session Guide

Signature pedagogy: Presentation. By the end of today, every student will have stood up and given a 90-second pitch of a personal venture vision — and learnt that vision is a verb, not a noun.

Phase-by-Phase Facilitation

  1. Open · 10 minHook: Show the class three founder photographs (e.g. Anita Roddick, Yvon Chouinard, Whitney Wolfe Herd) without names. Ask: "What do these three have in common?" Reveal: each started a business because of something they refused to accept about the world. Today's question: What do you refuse to accept?
  2. Frame · 15 minPairs share one minute each on the pre-reading (Effectuation principles). Faculty cold-calls 2–3 pairs to surface "bird-in-hand" examples from their own lives — what means do they already have? Connect to why entrepreneurship is taught as a discipline (Drucker), not a personality.
  3. Explore · 20 minOpen the Unit 1 framework gallery on the projector. Students individually browse for 8 minutes and pick the two frameworks they expect to use in their vision pitch. Quick popcorn share: "Which two and why?" Dont resolve disagreements — let them play out as choice points.
  4. Apply · 60 minSolo (25 min): Each student drafts a personal venture vision using:
    · F5 Golden Circle — Why · How · What
    · F6 BHAG — one sentence describing what victory looks like in 10 years
    · F8 SMART — three SMART goals for the next 12 months

    Pods of 3 (35 min): Each student pitches for 90 seconds. Pod members give two pieces of feedback only: (a) one strength, (b) one specific thing missing. Rotate. Three rounds total.
  5. Synthesise · 20 minEach pod nominates the pitch that surprised them most (not necessarily the "best" — surprise is the signal). Three nominees pitch to the full class. Class votes on which Effectuation principle was strongest in each.
  6. Reflect · 15 minSolo writing (in notebook or shared doc):
    · What was easier than expected?
    · What was harder than expected?
    · If you only had €500 and 30 days to start, what would you do tomorrow morning?
  7. Close · 10 minFaculty preview Unit 2 (Idea Triage) and assign:
    · Pre-reading: Unit 2 Pre-Reading
    · Reflection: Founder DNA
    · Bring three candidate business ideas to Unit 2.

Differentiation Notes

For students who freeze on "vision"

Pivot them to F4 Effectuation · bird-in-hand. Ask: "What three things — skills, contacts or resources — could you actually start with on Monday?" Vision can come after the first action.

For confident students

Push them up Bloom's ladder: ask for two alternative visions and a written rationale for which is more likely to find a willing customer in 6 weeks.
