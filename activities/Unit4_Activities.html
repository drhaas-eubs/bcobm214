BCOBM214 · Unit 4 · Session Activity

Strategy Sprint — Hackathon Session Guide

Signature pedagogy: Hackathon. By the end of today, every student will have drafted a one-page Business Model Canvas plus a 90-second strategic pitch — and learnt that strategy is what survives 90 minutes of constraint.

Phase-by-Phase Facilitation

  1. Open · 10 minHook: Show the class a 30-second clip of a Demo Day pitch where the founder pivots mid-pitch. Ask: "What did you notice?" Land the answer: strategy under time pressure exposes whether the team has actually decided. Today, each pod will face the same compression.
  2. Frame · 10 minFaculty draws the 9-block Business Model Canvas (F25) on the board. One minute on each block. Then introduces Mintzberg's 5 Ps (F27) as a meta-lens: every BMC implicitly uses all five Ps. Land the question: can your team produce one coherent BMC + go-to-market in 70 minutes?
  3. Explore · 15 minStudents individually browse the Unit 4 framework gallery for 8 minutes. Pick the three frameworks they expect to lean on. Quick popcorn share (5 min): which three and why?
  4. Apply · 70 min — The Strategy SprintForm pods of 3 (~4 pods of 3 for a class of 12). Each pod receives:
    · One industry card (e.g., urban micro-mobility, B2B SaaS for hospitality, sustainable fashion resale)
    · One constraint card (e.g., €30k budget, must launch in 90 days, regulatory uncertainty)
    · Six A3 sheets, post-its, markers

    Round 1 (20 min): Draft the Business Model Canvas (F25). All 9 blocks must be filled.
    Round 2 (20 min): Apply Hambrick & Fredrickson's Strategy Diamond (F28) — answer all five questions explicitly.
    Round 3 (20 min): Choose go-to-market mix (F29 4Ps or F30 7Ps depending on offer) and beachhead segment (F32 Bowling Alley).
    Wrap (10 min): Draft a 90-second pitch capturing the strategy. One person speaks, two answer questions.
  5. Synthesise · 20 minPlenary. Each pod delivers a 5-minute slot: 90-second pitch, 3-minute Q&A from the rest of the class. Faculty captures recurring patterns on the board: which frameworks survived contact with constraint? Which were paid lip service?
  6. Reflect · 10 minSolo writing:
    · What did your pod decide that turned out to be the most important call?
    · What did the constraint card force you to leave out — and was that a feature or a bug?
    · What would you change in the BMC if you had another 70 minutes?
  7. Close · 15 minFaculty preview Unit 5 (Plan Build Lab — moving from canvas to plan). Pre-reading: Six patterns behind failed plans. Bring your pod's BMC for Unit 5 plan-building work.

Differentiation Notes

For pods that get stuck

Instruct them to skip ahead to the pitch (Round 3) and reverse-engineer the BMC from there. Often the pitch surfaces the strategic clarity the canvas was masking.

For confident pods

Layer in F22 (Four Actions Grid) as a Round 4 challenge: which industry factor will they Eliminate, Reduce, Raise or Create to enter as a Blue Ocean rather than Red?
