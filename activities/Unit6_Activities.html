BCOBM214 · Unit 6 · Session Activity

Three Horizons — Case Conference

Signature pedagogy: Case Study. Today, students role-play a corporate innovation portfolio review board — deciding which initiatives belong in Horizon 1 (core), Horizon 2 (emerging), or Horizon 3 (future), and what gets killed.

The Four Roles

RoleBrief
CFOPrimary loyalty: shareholder return next quarter. Will defend H1 spend; sceptical of H3 commitments. Briefed with current P&L and capital position.
Chief Innovation OfficerPrimary loyalty: H2 and H3 health. Will argue that H1 over-investment is destroying future option value. Briefed with three-horizon spend ratio and competitor benchmarks.
IntrapreneurPitching an H3 initiative they want funded. Briefed with the initiative's lean canvas, riskiest assumptions, and 90-day milestones. Briefed also with Pinchot's 10 Commandments.
CEO (chair)Must integrate the three perspectives and make a binding portfolio call by minute 70. Briefed with the firm's 5-year strategic intent.

Phase-by-Phase Facilitation

  1. Open · 10 minHook: Show two photographs side-by-side: Kodak (peak 1996, gone 2012) and Fujifilm (still thriving). Ask: "Same industry. Same disruption. Same starting point. Why one outcome?" Land the answer: Fujifilm explicitly invested in Box 2 (forgetting) and Box 3 (creating), Kodak did not.
  2. Frame · 15 minFaculty draws Three Horizons (F43) on the board: H1 core, H2 emerging, H3 future. Then introduces Govindarajan's 3-Box Solution (F42) and Pinchot's 10 Commandments (F41). Today's case: students will sit on a portfolio review board with diverging incentives.
  3. Explore · 15 minStudents read the Helvetia Industries case individually. Pre-form their own answer to the question: which of three competing initiatives gets funded?
  4. Apply · 70 min — The BoardroomFor 12 students: form 3 boards of 4. Each board has CFO, CIO, Intrapreneur, CEO.

    Round 1 (15 min): Each role reads their private brief. CEO opens the meeting.
    Round 2 (25 min): Intrapreneur pitches the H3 initiative for 10 minutes. CFO & CIO question for 15 minutes.
    Round 3 (20 min): Boardroom debate. CFO and CIO argue portfolio allocation across H1/H2/H3. Intrapreneur defends the initiative.
    Round 4 (10 min): CEO makes the call. Funded? Modified? Killed? With what conditions?
  5. Synthesise · 15 minPlenary. Each board's CEO reports the verdict. Faculty captures patterns: which boards funded H3 generously? Which killed it? What argument tipped each call? Land the recurring tension: rational H1-defence in any single quarter is what makes Kodaks.
  6. Reflect · 10 minSolo writing:
    · If you were the intrapreneur whose initiative was killed, what would you do next Monday morning?
    · If you were the CFO whose H1 case was over-ruled, what's the most damaging consequence you'd watch for?
    · What would have to be true about your own firm for you to take on the Intrapreneur role?
  7. Close · 15 minFaculty preview Unit 7 (Growth Boardroom Role Play — same boardroom-as-classroom format, different problem). Pre-reading: When the founder becomes the bottleneck.
