The Pitch You Almost Made

Three independent-study prompts. ~30 minutes. Bring written responses (and your final-round pitch) to Unit 10 capstone.

Question 1 — The arc of three rounds

Compare your Round 1 pitch to your Round 3 pitch. In 6-8 sentences:

  • What changed in content?
  • What changed in delivery?
  • Which change was harder to make — content or delivery — and why?
  • If you ran a Round 4, what would change next?

Why this matters

Most founders pitch only once before the moment that matters. The few who pitch many times — to friends, to coaches, to small groups, to peers — arrive at the high-stakes pitch with iteration the others lack. Today's session was your version of that practice.

Question 2 — The feedback that landed

Of the feedback you received from your triad, what was the single comment that most changed your pitch? In 4-6 sentences:

  • Quote the comment
  • Explain why it worked when other comments didn't
  • What does this tell you about the feedback you should seek before a real high-stakes pitch?

Question 3 — The pitch you almost made

Most refined pitches are also somewhat sanitised pitches. The version that lands with investors is rarely the version the founder originally wanted to give — there is usually a more honest, more personal, more vulnerable version that the founder shaped into something safer. In 8-10 sentences:

  • Describe the more honest pitch you almost made
  • What did you sand off in pursuit of investor-readiness?
  • Was the trade-off worth it? When would it not be?
  • Is there a setting (Demo Day? capstone? a TED talk?) where the more honest version would actually work better?

How this connects forward

Unit 10 capstone is your final pitch — the integration of everything from Units 1-9 into a single 8-minute presentation in front of a real audience. The more honest version of your pitch may belong there. Bring both versions to capstone preparation; you'll choose deliberately.
