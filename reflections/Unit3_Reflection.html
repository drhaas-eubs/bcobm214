BCOBM214 · Unit 3 · Reflection

What You Didn't Expect to Hear

Three independent-study prompts. ~30 minutes. Bring written responses to Session 4.

Question 1 — The surprise

From your role play and your two real customer conversations, what is the one thing you heard that surprised you most? Quote it directly. Then write 4–6 sentences on what it changes about your idea.

Why this matters

Surprise is the cheapest form of validated learning. If you weren't surprised by anything, the conversation was probably a pitch in disguise.

Question 2 — The job

State, in one sentence, the job-to-be-done you now believe your customer is hiring your product to do. Use the format:

"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]."

Then write 4–6 sentences on whether your product, as currently designed, actually does that job better than the alternatives the customer is already using (including doing nothing).

Question 3 — The honest SOM

Sketch your TAM-SAM-SOM with three numbers. Then answer honestly:

  • How did you arrive at the SOM number?
  • If a sceptical investor asked you to defend it in 30 seconds, what would you say?
  • What would you need to test to make the SOM number believable?

How this connects forward

Unit 4 (Strategy Sprint Hackathon) demands a coherent business model. The quality of your Unit 4 work will trace directly back to whether your job-to-be-done is sharp and your SOM number is honest. There are no shortcuts — and there are no penalties for an honest "I don't know yet" if it's followed by "but here's the test."
