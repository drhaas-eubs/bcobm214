BCOBM214 · Unit 10 · Closing Reflection

From Student to Entrepreneur

Three closing prompts for independent reflection after the capstone. ~45 minutes. This reflection is not graded — it is yours, for the journey ahead.

Question 1 — The seven framework Slibraries that mattered most

Across 80 frameworks in 28 hours, a few will stay with you and most will fade from memory. In 8-10 sentences:

  • Identify the seven framework Slibraries (your "personal seven") you most expect to use in the next two years
  • For each, write one sentence on why it earned its place
  • Reflect: what does the selection say about the kind of entrepreneur you are becoming?

Why this matters

Frameworks are not neutral tools. The frameworks you reach for shape the questions you ask and the decisions you make. The act of choosing your personal seven is itself a strategic act.

Question 2 — The capstone you delivered

Look back at your capstone pitch. In 8-10 sentences:

  • What part of the pitch are you most proud of?
  • What part of the pitch did the panel see through that you hoped they would not?
  • Which framework's lens did you most use in your own pitch — and which did you most need from the audience?
  • If you delivered the same pitch in 12 months, what would change?

Question 3 — From student to entrepreneur

This is the closing question of the course. In 12-15 sentences (or as many as it takes to be honest):

  • When you started this course, what did you believe entrepreneurship was?
  • What do you believe it is now?
  • Has the course made you more or less likely to start a venture in the next five years? What changed?
  • If you don't start a venture, what from this course will you carry into whatever you do?
  • If you do start a venture, what is the first thing you will do on the Monday after starting?

The closing principle

Sarasvathy's Pilot-in-the-Plane (F80) is the framework this course closes with. It says: the future of your venture is not predicted; it is co-created. You stay at the controls. Whatever you decide to build, build it with stakeholders willing to come along, with the means already in your hands, with affordable downside, and with the willingness to use surprise as material.

That is the entrepreneurial stance. We have spent 28 hours building it. The rest is yours.

One last thing — staying in touch

The frameworks live online at drhaas-eubs.github.io/bcobm214. The Slibrary sheets are downloadable as PDFs. Authors of every framework are credited in the Author Slibrary. Use them. Build with them. And when you build something interesting, write — hildegard.haas@euruni.edu. Watching you build is the reason this course exists.
