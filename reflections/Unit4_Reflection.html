BCOBM214 · Unit 4 · Reflection

The Plan vs The Planning

Three independent-study prompts. ~30 minutes. Bring written responses to Session 5.

Question 1 — What survived the timer

Look back at your pod's Strategy Sprint output. Of the nine BMC blocks you filled in 20 minutes, which two would you most aggressively defend in front of an investor? Which two would you most readily change? Write 6-8 sentences explaining the asymmetry — why are some choices stronger than others?

Why this matters

The BMC's value isn't that all blocks are equally strong. The value is that you can see which blocks are strong and which are placeholders. Most plans hide that asymmetry; the canvas exposes it.

Question 2 — The constraint card's gift

Your pod was forced to make the strategy work within a constraint (budget, timeline, regulation). In 4-6 sentences, describe how the constraint changed the strategy versus what you would have produced without it. Was the constrained version better, worse, or just different — and why?

Question 3 — The plan vs the planning

Eisenhower famously said: "Plans are worthless, but planning is everything." After today's hackathon, write 6-10 sentences on what you think he meant. In particular:

  • What did the act of planning produce that the plan itself does not capture?
  • What are you taking forward into Unit 5 (Plan Build Lab) that you didn't have at 9am today?
  • If your venture had to pivot tomorrow, which part of today's plan would you keep — and which would you throw out?

How this connects forward

Unit 5 (Plan Build Lab) takes today's BMC and converts it into a structured business plan using Sahlman's frame. The quality of Unit 5 depends almost entirely on the honesty of today's reflection. Bring it.
