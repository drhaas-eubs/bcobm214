BCOBM214 · Unit 2 · Reflection

Killing Your Darlings

Three independent-study prompts. ~30 minutes. Bring written responses to Session 3.

Question 1 — The idea you almost kept

In Session 2, your triad helped you set aside two of your three ideas. Pick the one that was hardest to let go. In 6–8 sentences, write:

  • What attracted you to it?
  • What was the strongest argument for keeping it?
  • What was the strongest argument for killing it?
  • What would have to change in the world for you to revive it?

Question 2 — The first sign

For the idea you are keeping, write the answer to the Unit 2 reading prompt:

If this idea is going to fail, what is the very first sign?

Be specific. "No one buys it" is not a first sign — that's the last sign. A first sign is something subtle and early: a low click-through rate, a polite but vague reply, a friend whose enthusiasm doesn't survive the third question.

Question 3 — Your customer hunter mission

By Session 3 you must have spoken to two potential customers about your surviving idea. Before you do, write:

  • Who exactly will you speak to (name them, not segments)?
  • How will you reach them in the next 5 days?
  • What will you not say (because of the Mom Test)?
  • What three questions will you ask?

Why this matters

Unit 3 expects you to arrive with insights from real conversations. The quality of those insights traces back almost entirely to the quality of the questions you write down before the conversation. Founders who improvise discovery interviews learn how convincing they themselves are. Founders who plan discovery interviews learn how the customer thinks.
