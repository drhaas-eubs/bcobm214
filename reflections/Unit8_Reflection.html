BCOBM214 · Unit 8 · Reflection

Whose Money Are You Taking?

Three independent-study prompts. ~30 minutes. Bring written responses to Session 9.

Question 1 — The constraint that found you

In Session 8 your pod drew a constraint card at random. Write 6-8 sentences:

  • Restate the constraint
  • Describe how the constraint changed the funding plan you would have written without it
  • Reflect: are real ventures usually facing one constraint, or several at once?
  • If your constraint were doubled in severity, would the plan still work?

Why this matters

Real founders rarely face the funding question in the abstract. They face it inside a constraint — a co-founder leaving, a market shift, a key customer leaving, a regulatory event. The discipline is to plan inside reality, not inside theory.

Question 2 — The dilution you accepted

For your pod's chosen funding strategy, your founder ownership at month 24 is some specific number. In 4-6 sentences:

  • State the number
  • What did you trade away in exchange for that dilution? (Not just cash — also: time, control, optionality, brand.)
  • Was the trade fair? At what dilution would the trade have been unfair?

Question 3 — Whose money are you taking?

Capital is never just capital. It comes from someone, with their incentives, time horizon and worldview attached. In 8-10 sentences:

  • For your venture concept, name the type of investor whose worldview is most aligned with what you're trying to build (and why)
  • Name the type of investor whose money you would refuse, even if offered, and why
  • If you had to take only one of three options — bootstrap forever, take family money, take VC — which would you pick for your specific venture, and what does the answer tell you about your venture's true nature?

How this connects forward

Unit 9 (Pitch Craft Studio) puts you in front of investors directly. The pitches you draft will be sharpened by the funding clarity you build today. The single most common pitch failure is incoherence between the venture's economics and the capital being requested — Unit 8's discipline is what prevents it.
