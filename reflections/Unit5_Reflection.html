What Can't Be in the Plan

Three independent-study prompts. ~30 minutes. Bring written responses to Session 6.

Question 1 — The unwritten assumption

Every plan rests on at least one assumption that the founder hasn't tested but cannot afford to be wrong about. Identify the unwritten assumption in your pod's plan. Write 6-8 sentences:

  • State the assumption explicitly
  • Explain why it didn't make it into the plan
  • Describe the smallest experiment that would test it in 14 days

Why this matters

The most dangerous assumptions are the ones the team agrees on without ever stating. Surfacing them in writing is the cheapest pre-mortem available.

Question 2 — The peer audit you trusted

In Session 5, another pod audited your plan for Eisenmann's six patterns. Of their findings, which one do you most disagree with — and which one do you secretly suspect is correct? In 4-6 sentences, explain the difference between the two responses. (The answer is rarely about evidence; it's usually about identity — which finding threatens what you've already committed to.)

Question 3 — What can't be in the plan

Some things genuinely cannot be planned: founder fit, team dynamics under stress, the right hire showing up at the right moment, the customer who unexpectedly becomes an evangelist. In 8-10 sentences:

  • List 3-5 things you believe matter for your venture's success but cannot be captured in the plan
  • For each, describe how you would recognise it if it happened
  • Reflect: does that mean the plan is incomplete, or that the plan and the planning serve different purposes?

How this connects forward

Unit 6 (Intrapreneurship case conference) shifts perspective from independent founders to entrepreneurs operating inside large corporations. The unwritten-assumption discipline you build today transfers directly: corporate plans hide more assumptions than startup plans, and the consequences of leaving them unspoken are larger.
