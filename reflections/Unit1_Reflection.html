BCOBM214 · Unit 1 · Reflection

Founder DNA

Three independent-study questions for the week between Unit 1 and Unit 2. ~30 minutes of writing. Bring your responses to Session 2.

Question 1 — Bird in Hand

List three of each, honestly:

  • Three things you know that most of your peers don't
  • Three people you could call tomorrow who would take your call
  • Three things you have right now (skills, money, equipment, time, an unfair advantage)

Now look at the nine items. Circle any unexpected combinations. What kind of business could those circled items, alone, support? Write 4–6 sentences.

Question 2 — Affordable Loss

If you started a venture in the next 60 days, what is the most you could afford to lose — in money, in time, and in reputation — without it being catastrophic? Write a single number for each. Be specific.

Why this matters

Causal entrepreneurs ask "what's the expected return?" Effectual entrepreneurs ask "what's the affordable loss?" The second question is harder to dodge.

Question 3 — The Founder You're Not (Yet)

Choose one of the four entrepreneurship lenses we encountered (Schumpeter, Kirzner, Drucker, Sarasvathy). Write 6–10 sentences arguing the lens that least describes you today — and what it would take, concretely, to grow into it by the end of this course.

How this connects forward

Your responses become raw material for Unit 2 (Idea Triage). You will arrive at Session 2 with three candidate ideas; the depth of those ideas usually traces back to the depth of this reflection.
