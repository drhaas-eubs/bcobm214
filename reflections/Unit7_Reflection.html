BCOBM214 · Unit 7 · Reflection

Letting Go to Grow

Three independent-study prompts. ~30 minutes. Bring written responses to Session 8.

Question 1 — The seat you sat in

You played one of five roles in the Olive Lab boardroom (founder-CEO, lead investor, independent director, co-founder/CTO, head of growth). Write 6-8 sentences:

  • What surprised you about the experience of advocating for that role's interest?
  • What argument did you find yourself making that you don't actually believe outside the role?
  • If the role were yours in real life, what additional information would you want before the next board meeting?

Why this matters

Boardroom decisions are rarely about the data on the table. They are about the perspectives, incentives, and trust relationships of the people in the seats. Inhabiting a role for 75 minutes makes the texture of those dynamics visible.

Question 2 — The verdict you reached

Your board reached a decision (founder stays, founder evolves, professional CEO recruited). In 4-6 sentences:

  • What does the verdict cost the company and what does it cost the founder?
  • What was the strongest argument against the verdict that didn't get enough air time?
  • How would you communicate the decision to a junior team member who hadn't been in the room?

Question 3 — Letting go to grow

Most founders identify deeply with their venture. "Letting go" — of decisions, of authority, of identity-as-CEO — is one of the hardest acts in entrepreneurial life. In 8-10 sentences, reflect:

  • Have you experienced an analogous "letting go" moment in your own life (a club role, a project lead, a hand-over to a successor)? What made it hard?
  • What did you keep that you should have given up? What did you give up that you should have kept?
  • If your future entrepreneurial self faced the Léa moment, what would your past self most need to remember?

How this connects forward

Unit 8 (Funding Constraint Cards Hackathon) shifts attention to the financial dimension of growth. The boardroom you sat in today was wrestling not only with leadership — it was wrestling with whether the firm could raise a Series B successfully. Tomorrow's hackathon takes that funding question apart in detail.
